Itinerary
10:00
Takayama Jinya & Machiya Walk
Architecture

Your guide leads you through the Jinya — the best-preserved Edo-period government building in Japan — and into the old merchant district. You'll learn how Hida's master carpenters built without a single nail, using interlocking joints that have held for centuries. The same techniques that raised Nara's great temples were born in this mountain town.

[ Photo: Jinya / Machiya ]
11:00
Meet the Wood Artists
Workshop visit

Inside the Jinya grounds and the surrounding lanes, working artisans keep the craft alive. You'll meet woodcarvers practicing Ichi-i Ittōbori — single-knife carving from yew — and see the intricate process up close. These are not demonstrations for tourists. This is how they work every day.

[ Photo: Wood artist ]
12:00
Craft Shop Tour — Ichi-i Ittōbori & Shunkei
Shopping

A walk through Takayama's finest craft shops. Your guide explains what separates a master's work from an imitation — the grain, the finish, the weight of a well-made piece. Shunkei lacquerware glows amber under the light. Woodcarvings that took weeks to complete sit quietly in small storefronts that have been here for generations.

[ Photo: Craft shops ]
13:00
Lunch — self-guided
Free time

Time on your own. We'll suggest a few spots nearby — from quiet soba shops to riverside cafés. Reconvene at 14:30 for the afternoon experience.

14:30
Shunkei Lacquerware Experience
Workshop

In a working lacquerware studio, you'll try your hand at Shunkei — Takayama's signature style of natural lacquer over pale wood. The grain shows through, warm and luminous. A craftsperson guides you through the process, and the piece you make goes home with you. About one hour.

[ Photo: Shunkei lacquerware ]
16:00
200-Year Machiya — Architecture & Tea
Cultural

The day ends in a beautifully preserved townhouse that has stood for two centuries. Your guide walks you through the construction — the dark timber frame, the interlocking joints, the earthen walls. Then tea, quietly, in a room that has seen a lot of history.

[ Photo: Machiya interior ]

Day 1 ends
Finish

Furniture Factory Visit — Morning
Factory tour

Hida is home to some of Japan's most celebrated furniture makers. Your first factory visit shows the full process — from raw timber to finished chair. You'll see craftspeople working with the same care and precision as the temple builders of a thousand years ago, now making objects for modern homes around the world.

[ Photo: Furniture factory ]

Lunch

Second Furniture Factory Visit
Factory tour

A second factory, a different philosophy. Hida's furniture makers each have their own approach to wood — grain selection, joinery, finish. By seeing two in one day, you begin to understand what makes Hida furniture genuinely special in a way that words alone can't convey.

Wood Craft Workshop
Workshop

Your turn to work with wood. In a hands-on workshop, you'll craft a small object — a cutting board, a small tray, or a similar piece — guided by a craftsperson who will show you the basics of shaping and finishing. What you make, you take home.

Frequently asked questions
Shunkei is Takayama's signature lacquerware style, developed in the 16th century. Unlike most lacquer — which covers and conceals — Shunkei uses a translucent natural lacquer that lets the wood grain show through. The result is warm, luminous, and entirely unique to this region. In the workshop, a craftsperson will guide you through applying the lacquer to your own piece, which you take home when it's done.
On the evening of Day 1, we can arrange a visit to a pottery workshop about 10 minutes from central Takayama. You'll see the kiln, learn about the local ceramic tradition, and paint your own piece — which is then fired and sent to you at home, wherever in the world you are. The cost is ¥40,000 per person and is booked separately. Please let us know at the time of booking if you're interested.
Hida is home to several world-class furniture manufacturers whose pieces appear in homes and hotels across Japan and beyond. We visit two factories on Day 2, chosen for the contrast in their approach to wood. The specific factories may vary depending on availability and season — we'll confirm your itinerary in advance. Factory tours are not generally open to the public; this access is arranged specifically for our guests.
You'll choose from a small object or a cutting board, guided by a craftsperson who will teach you the basics of shaping and finishing Hida wood. No prior experience is needed — the process is designed so anyone can produce something genuinely beautiful in about 90 minutes. Everything you make comes home with you.
This tour runs year-round. Spring and autumn are the most popular — the old town is beautiful in cherry blossom season and in the golden light of October. Winter has its own appeal: snow on the machiya rooftops, warm workshops, and far fewer visitors. Summer is green and atmospheric. The craft workshops and factory visits are indoors, so weather has little effect on the experience.
