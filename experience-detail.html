The Place
The Place

A home that has stood since the Edo period.

The machiya sits on the main street of Takayama's Historic Old Town, one of the best-preserved Edo-period streetscapes in Japan.

Built over 200 years ago, it has been a family home ever since. The same dark timber, the same narrow facade, the same smell of old wood and tatami.

The Hosts
The Hosts

A local family
who love to cook.

This is not a demonstration. You cook with them — at their table, with their knives, with the ingredients they chose that morning at the market.

By the time you sit down to eat, the conversation has already started.

The Centrepiece

A5 Hida Wagyu.

The highest grade.

Hida Wagyu is among the most celebrated beef in Japan. A5 is its highest grade — achieved by only a small percentage of cattle. You'll prepare it together as part of a 4-course menu that also features fresh seasonal ingredients from the local market.

Dashi. Knife skills. The fundamentals of Japanese cuisine — and then the meal you made with your own hands.
