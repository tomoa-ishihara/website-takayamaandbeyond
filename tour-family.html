Itinerary
9:00
Takayama Morning Market
Food walk

We start at Jinya-mae Morning Market — one of Japan's oldest. Stalls of pickled vegetables, fresh tofu, local miso, and mountain honey line the riverbank. Your guide introduces the vendors, and kids get to taste as they go. Expect sticky fingers by 9:30.

10:00
Sanmachi Suji Food Walk
Food walk

Through Takayama's beautifully preserved merchant district — mitarashi dango fresh off the grill, hida beef skewers, mitarashi-flavoured soft serve. Your guide picks the best stops for kids and tells the stories behind each one. The whole family eats well.

12:00
Free time — lunch & rest
Self-guided

This time is yours. If you're still full from the walk, take a stroll along the Miyagawa river and let the kids play by the water. If you're hungry, grab lunch at one of our recommended spots nearby. We reconvene at 1:30 — rested and ready to make things.

13:30
Food Sample Workshop
Workshop

Japan's famous plastic food displays — but you make them yourself. Using coloured wax, you craft hyper-realistic replicas of Japanese dishes: sushi, parfait, ice cream, whatever you choose. Every piece goes home with you as a keepsake. They can't be eaten, but they last forever — and they look astonishingly real.

16:00
Cooking Experience in a 200-Year Machiya
Cooking

Inside a beautifully preserved 200-year-old townhouse, your family cooks a 5-course dinner together — including Hida beef. Every dish is designed so children can lead the preparation. Along the way you'll learn how to make dashi from scratch using kombu and katsuobushi, understand the difference between light and dark soy sauce, and see how these fundamentals shape everything in Japanese cooking. Food education at its most hands-on — and at the end, you eat what you made.

19:00
Tour ends — full and happy
Finish

We wrap up at 7pm in central Takayama, a short walk from most hotels. The kids will sleep well tonight.
